============================================================
  HOW TO ADD REAL SOUND EFFECTS & MUSIC TO YOUR GAME
============================================================

The game is fully wired for audio — it just needs real sound
files. The SoundManager gracefully skips any missing files,
so the game works fine without them.


STEP 1: Get Sound Files
------------------------
You need 8 audio files in .ogg format (recommended) or .mp3:

  swap.ogg      — Short "swoosh" sound when two gems swap
  match.ogg     — Satisfying "pop" or "ding" when gems match
  cascade.ogg   — Quick "tumble" sound as gems fall down
  special.ogg   — Powerful "boom" or "sparkle" for special gem activation
  win.ogg       — Victory fanfare when you complete a level
  lose.ogg      — Sad/disappointed sound for game over
  shuffle.ogg   — "Whoosh" or shuffle sound when the board reshuffles
  bgm.ogg       — Background music loop (can be longer, 30-120 seconds)

Where to find free game sounds:
  - https://freesound.org (free with attribution)
  - https://opengameart.org (free game assets)
  - https://pixabay.com/sound-effects (royalty-free)
  - https://sfxr.me (generate retro sound effects in your browser!)
  - Search "free game sound effects" on YouTube for tutorials


STEP 2: Place Files in the Project
------------------------------------
Copy your .ogg files into this folder:

  app/src/main/res/raw/

So the full paths will be:
  app/src/main/res/raw/swap.ogg
  app/src/main/res/raw/match.ogg
  app/src/main/res/raw/cascade.ogg
  app/src/main/res/raw/special.ogg
  app/src/main/res/raw/win.ogg
  app/src/main/res/raw/lose.ogg
  app/src/main/res/raw/shuffle.ogg
  app/src/main/res/raw/bgm.ogg

IMPORTANT: File names must be lowercase, no spaces, no
special characters. Only letters, numbers, and underscores.


STEP 3: Rebuild the App
-------------------------
After adding the files:
  1. In Android Studio: Build > Rebuild Project
  2. Run the app on your device/emulator
  3. The sounds should play automatically!

No code changes needed — the SoundManager detects the files
at runtime using their names.


STEP 4 (Optional): Adjust Volume & Behavior
---------------------------------------------
If you want to tweak audio settings, edit:
  app/src/main/java/com/galaxymatch/game/audio/SoundManager.kt

Key things you can change:
  - Background music volume: line with setVolume(0.4f, 0.4f)
    Change 0.4f to 0.0f (silent) through 1.0f (full volume)

  - Combo pitch escalation: line with (1.0f + comboLevel * 0.1f)
    Change 0.1f to make pitch rise faster/slower per combo

  - Mute sounds programmatically:
    ServiceLocator.soundManager.isSfxMuted = true    // Mute effects
    ServiceLocator.soundManager.isMusicMuted = true   // Mute music


WHEN EACH SOUND PLAYS
-----------------------
  swap.ogg      → Player swipes to swap two gems
  match.ogg     → Matched gems flash/highlight before clearing
  cascade.ogg   → After clearing, gems fall and new ones enter
                   (pitch rises with each chain combo!)
  special.ogg   → A special gem (striped/wrapped/bomb) activates
  win.ogg       → Player reaches the target score (level complete)
  lose.ogg      → Player runs out of moves (game over)
  shuffle.ogg   → No valid moves available, board reshuffles
  bgm.ogg       → Loops continuously from splash screen onward,
                   pauses when app goes to background


TIPS
-----
  - Keep sound effect files SHORT (under 2 seconds)
  - Background music should loop cleanly (fade out = fade in)
  - .ogg format is preferred over .mp3 for Android (smaller, better quality)
  - Test on a real device — emulator audio can be laggy
